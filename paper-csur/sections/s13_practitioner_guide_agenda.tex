\section{Practitioner Guide, Related Surveys, and Research Agenda}
\label{sec:practitioner}

This section turns the taxonomy into a mechanism-selection rule, positions the survey against eighteen neighboring frameworks, and distills the open questions exposed by the synthesis.

\subsection{Choosing a Verbal-Feedback Mechanism}
\label{subsec:mechanism}

Choose the feedback form before the optimizer. Four questions determine the mechanism in Figure~\ref{fig:decision-tree}. First, are model weights accessible? If so, use a compiled reward or rubric when outcomes are mechanically verifiable, monitoring objective hacking; otherwise, if verbal feedback is plentiful, train on it directly and monitor sycophancy and filter bias \citep{luo2025fcp}. Second, if weights are inaccessible but latency permits iteration, use deliberative feedback and ground the critic in tools or tests rather than relying on circular self-critique \citep{kamoi2024can,gou2024critic}. Third, when iteration is too costly but tasks recur, persist distilled lessons and audit memory for stale or poisoned entries. Finally, if none of these preconditions holds, invest in grounding the task, state, or action interface before building a feedback loop. Each choice fixes a dominant risk as well as an optimization family.

\subsection{Comparison with Prior Surveys}
\label{subsec:priorsurveys}

To our knowledge, this survey is the first organized around the verbal signal itself: which component of the decision problem a linguistic artifact modifies and when it takes effect. Table~\ref{tab:surveys} makes the positioning explicit against eighteen prior surveys and frameworks. Most shelf neighbors organize by mechanism (correction timing, evolution phase, rollout stage), by agent component (memory, capability, module under optimization), or by algorithm family (RLHF, preference optimization, agentic RL); the nearest exception, the feedback typing of \citet{fernandes2023bridging}, does take the signal as its axis but predates the agentic signals surveyed here. None combines a signal-centric axis with a formal framework, coverage of all three stages (problem definition, inference, and training), and treatment of the feedback channel's safety; that fourfold combination is the position this survey occupies.

\subsection{Research Agenda}
\label{subsec:agenda}

The synthesis exposes ten linked gaps. Theory must identify what language contributes beyond scalarization, while systems work must make feedback trustworthy and usable.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
    \item Characterize when verbal feedback is more sample efficient than scalar reward and extend current separations to intermediate formats \citep{xu2025formalizing}.
    \item Establish convergence conditions for compiled and rubric-shaped rewards under objective mis-specification.
    \item Assign free-form critiques to the responsible spans, steps, and decisions rather than only to outcomes \citep{wang2026text2gradreinforcementlearningnatural}.
    \item Measure information lost when a critique is compressed to a preference label or score.
    \item Authenticate feedback provenance and evaluate adaptive injection, memory poisoning, and reward manipulation \citep{zhan2025adaptive}.
    \item Report feedback quality separately from the receiving model's ability to use accurate feedback \citep{jiang2025feedback}.
    \item Train calibrated critics for error localization and actionable correction instead of reusing the generator uncritically \citep{mcaleese2024criticgpt}.
    \item Standardize machine-readable tool feedback that separates task errors from infrastructure failures.
    \item Formalize when persistent memory or replay crosses the boundary from inference to training.
    \item Test transfer, validity, and affected-party harms in embodied and high-stakes deployments \citep{sharma2024towards}.
\end{enumerate}
