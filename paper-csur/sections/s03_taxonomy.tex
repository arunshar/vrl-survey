\section{Taxonomy}
\label{sec:taxonomy}

The framework of \S\ref{sec:framework} identifies where a linguistic artifact can enter an agent. We organize qualifying signals by \emph{when} they take effect and \emph{what} they modify, yielding the three pillars in Figure~\ref{fig:taxonomy}. The pillars classify signals, not systems: one agent may consume several roles at once. They are not lifecycle stages, and the definitional fence still applies at every leaf. In particular, grounding must feed an improvement loop, while scalar-only preference optimization remains outside VRL.

The dashed preference-shaping leaf is outside the VRL fence because the linguistic judgment is scalarized before learning.}
    \label{fig:taxonomy}
\end{figure}

The leaves name one representative anchor per mechanism: SayCan \citep{ahn2022can}, Closed-Loop State Feedback \citep{bhat2024grounding}, Inner Monologue \citep{huang2022inner}, Eureka \citep{ma2024eureka}, Self-Refine \citep{madaan2023selfrefine}, ChatEval \citep{chan2023chatevalbetterllmbasedevaluators}, Reflexion \citep{shinn2023reflexion}, Tree of Thoughts \citep{yao2023tree}, FCP \citep{luo2025fcp}, STaR \citep{zelikman2022star}, DeepCritic \citep{yang2025deepcritic}, MALT \citep{motwani2024malt}, GenPRM \citep{zhao2025genprm}, and DPO \citep{rafailov2023direct}.

\textbf{Grounding signals} act at problem-definition time and specify goals, state descriptions, action interfaces, or rewards. They qualify only when the constructed component subsequently drives revision or learning; a one-shot instruction to a frozen policy does not. Reward-code generation is the clearest case because language constructs the reward that trains the policy \citep{ma2024eureka}. \textbf{Deliberative feedback} acts at inference time through critique, memory retrieval, debate, or search, changing context or output without updating parameters \citep{madaan2023selfrefine,shinn2023reflexion}. \textbf{Learning signals} act at training time, where feedback changes parameters or training data and thus persists across future interactions \citep{zelikman2022star,yuan2024self}. Persistence distinguishes the roles: grounding defines the task, deliberation changes an episode or memory state, and learning changes the policy.

\subsection{An Explicit Assignment Rule for Hybrid Methods}
\label{subsec:assignment}

Hybrid methods require a reproducible placement procedure. We use one rule:
\begin{quote}
    \emph{A method is placed by what its linguistic signal modifies at the moment the signal takes effect.}
\end{quote}
Table~\ref{tab:hybrid} applies the rule and tags secondary roles rather than suppressing them. Reward code is grounding when language constructs the task objective, even though that objective later trains a policy. Retrieved experience is deliberative because it changes inference context, even when memory persists across episodes. Constitutional critique and feedback-conditioned fine-tuning are learning signals when their durable effect is a parameter update. The same system may therefore appear under several pillars, but each artifact receives one primary placement at each point of use.

The temporal axis is preferable to source or modality because identical language can have different effects at different points of use. ``The gripper pressure is too high'' can constrain a task, revise a plan, or shape a compiled reward. Grouping by effect keeps together methods that share failure modes: grounding fidelity, deliberative feedback quality, or loss of information during training-time scalarization.
