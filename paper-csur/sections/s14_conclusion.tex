\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This survey organizes verbal reinforcement learning by where a natural-language signal enters a decision problem and what it modifies when it takes effect. The resulting pillars separate grounding at problem-definition time, deliberative feedback during inference, and learning signals that persist through training. A two-criterion fence excludes plain instruction following and feedback scalarized before use, while the assignment rule places hybrid methods by each signal's point of effect. This signal-centric view connects topics often treated separately: reward construction, critique, memory, credit assignment, language-space optimization, evaluation, and feedback-channel safety.

The practical implication is that feedback format cannot be selected independently of its consumer. A verifier, free-form critique, stored lesson, and training rationale carry different information, require different credit mechanisms, and expose different attack surfaces. The field therefore needs theory that measures what survives scalarization, evaluations that separate feedback quality from utilization, and provenance-aware interfaces that make external feedback auditable. These priorities determine whether language becomes a dependable control signal rather than another unverified model output.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{subsec:limitations}

This article synthesizes representative work rather than exhaustively enumerating every system. Its taxonomy is an organizing lens, not a strict partition, because one system may consume several signal roles. Scope is restricted to explicit textual feedback; spoken and richer multimodal feedback fall outside the fence even when observations are visual. The survey contributes no new benchmark or controlled comparison, and formal guarantees remain localized. Evidence for real-world transfer is still sparse, especially in embodied and high-stakes settings, so successful demonstrations do not establish broad reliability. Where the literature supplies no guarantee or comparative evidence, we state the gap rather than infer a conclusion.
